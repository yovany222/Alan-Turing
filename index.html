Alan Turing

The Father of Modern Computing

Alan Turing, engrossed in his work, breaking codes and laying the foundations of computer science.

Here's a time line of Alan Turing's life:

  • 1912 - Born in London, England
  • 1931 - Begins studying at King's College, Cambridge, showing exceptional skills in mathematics
  • 1936 - Publishes "On Computable Numbers," introducing the concept of a universal machine, later known as the Turing Machine
  • 1938 - Earns his Ph.D. from Princeton University, where he expands his work on computability and cryptography
  • 1939 - Joins the British Government Code and Cypher School at Bletchley Park, works on breaking the German Enigma code during World War II
  • 1941 - Breaks the Enigma code, significantly contributing to the Allied war effort
  • 1945 - Receives the Order of the British Empire for his wartime services
  • 1946 - Designs the Automatic Computing Engine (ACE), a blueprint for one of the earliest stored-program computers
  • 1950 - Publishes "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," introducing the concept of the Turing Test to determine a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior
  • 1952 - Convicted for homosexuality, undergoes chemical castration as an alternative to prison
  • 1954 - Dies of cyanide poisoning, ruled as suicide, though some believe it may have been accidental

"Alan Turing's work not only helped win World War II but also laid the groundwork for modern computing and artificial intelligence. His legacy continues to influence technology and science to this day."

-- Tim Berners-Lee, Inventor of the World Wide Web
